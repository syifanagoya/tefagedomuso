What to Keep in Mind

Trends

Slouchy sweatshirts and tapered pants are quintessentially in the matching sweatsuits realm, but fresh iterations abound this season. “Wide-leg sweatpants are a new trend for both men and women,” notes Zapp. While cropped sweatshirts have recently taken center stage, more “cropped zip hoodies with matching sweatpants” are emerging in various solids, especially “neutrals and browns.”

As Grede highlights, the latest sweatsuits come in “vibrant colors, a mix of fabrics, and tasteful embellishments,” ensuring both style and fit. Torres adds that the styling game is changing, too, with sweatsuits paired with “chunky gold jewelry or a fitted button-down shirt” to capture the beloved streetwear-chic vibe.

Amidst these changes, the essence of sweats remains unchanged. Chan shares her timeless stance, preferring “standard athletic cotton ones or luxe, finely knit ones,” but isn't averse to embracing seasonal colors.

Fit

The ideal fit for a matching sweatsuit varies from wearer to wearer. However, Zapp says, “A slouchy fit is the ideal,” without compromising the option of layering. Chan emphasizes the role of tailoring in achieving the perfect fit. She points out that while the initial fit around areas like the waist and shoulders is crucial, “the rest can be fixed by alterations.” Many might overlook the idea of tailoring casual garments, but the model's perspective suggests that if you're wearing them frequently, it’s an investment worth making.

With inclusivity at the forefront of more and more brands like Good American, Grede highlights the label’s focus on a wide range of matching sweatsuit silhouettes that embrace all figures, including plus-size and curvy shapes, “ensuring every individual feels both stylish and comfortable without compromise.” Torres echoes this sentiment, emphasizing the importance of personal style over adhering to conventional rules. Recognizing the diversity in body types, she suggests mixing and matching to find the right balance. “If you happen to be smaller at the top and fuller at the bottom, opt for a slouchy crop and easy wide-leg bottom,” she says.

Fabric

The fabric of a matching sweatsuit plays a pivotal role in its comfort and appeal. Chan tends towards classic plush cotton for its familiar comfort and the luxury of super-soft cashmere. She also emphasizes the importance of sustainable choices, praising Lenzing EcoVera’s viscose for its eco-conscious properties, stretchability, and ability to wick away sweat. Torres, on the other hand, has a soft spot for French terry, citing its soft feel and durability that resists shrinking and fading, making it ideal for both vibrant hues and timeless dark neutrals. 

Your Questions, Answered

How do you style a matching sweatsuit?

Styling a matching sweatsuit has evolved beyond the boundaries of simple athleisure, making room for personal touches and unexpected combinations. Chan shares her unique approach, “It’s kind of like wrong shoe theory: when it finally hits, it hits. One of the ways I come up with ideas is to pretend that it’s a ‘normal’ tailored suit and go from there,” she says, once unexpectedly pairing her sweatsuit with a corset, a chain, pearl necklaces, and heels.

Grede's take revolves around the blend of comfort and style. “The key to styling a matching sweatsuit is all about embracing comfort while adding personal touches,” she emphasizes. Her tip? To stray beyond the expected, such as mixing in statement jewelry or swapping casual shoes for heeled booties. It can even be as simple as switching out your Apple watch band — chunky metal for sporty silicone. "Balancing proportions is key, especially with sweatpants,” she adds. “Pair looser bottoms with a more tapered top to maintain a casual yet refined look.”

Torres and Zapp echo the sentiment of playing with proportions to create a modern aesthetic balance. Torres recommends pairing contrasting fits, such as a “fitted sweatshirt with wide-legged sweatpants,” and suggests layering different fabrics, like a denim jacket, for added texture. Zapp takes a versatile approach, suggesting the classic sporty look with sneakers or a long trench and leather mules for a dressier feel.

Where can you wear a matching sweatsuit?

Where to wear a matching sweatsuit? The consensus tilts towards casual and cozy scenarios. Chan highlights typical places like “home, therapy, and traveling.” But she doesn’t hesitate to venture into unexpected territory, proposing a casual coffee meet-up or a relaxed date setting as ideal places.

However, not all settings lend themselves to the relaxed aesthetic of a sweatsuit. Chan warns against wearing them to traditional workplaces, especially outside of the realms of fashion, music, entertainment, or tech. Even within those industries, she suggests a touch of “cautionary judgment.” Torres shares this sentiment, recommending sweatsuits for casual endeavors like “running errands, working from home, casually getting together with friends or long flights.” But she also cautions against them in more formal dining scenarios, as dinners typically require a touch more elegance. While incorporating sweats into workwear is slowly becoming a trend, Torres emphasizes that this works best when combined with tailored pieces to strike a balance.

What is the most versatile matching sweatsuit?

The most versatile matching sweatsuit hinges predominantly on color and fabric. Chan champions “neutral colors” as the go-to for adaptability, while Torres says materials like “jersey, cotton, knit ribs, sweater, and French terry” offer both seasonality and versatility. Further reinforcing the power of neutrals, she recommends staples in black, white, cream, and brown, noting their effortless transition across varied looks. 

Similarly, Zapp emphasizes the adaptability of timeless colors, as they seamlessly pair with various layering pieces and accessories. Serious question: Do matching sweatsuits have to match exactly? Quick answer: Opinions vary.
